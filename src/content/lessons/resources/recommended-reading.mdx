# Recommended Reading

These books offer deeper exploration of the ideas introduced in this course. Each one approaches architecture from a slightly different angle — together, they provide a well-rounded foundation for continued learning.

## Core Recommendations

### 101 Things I Learned in Architecture School
**By Matthew Frederick**

This compact, illustrated book distills architectural thinking into 101 concise lessons. It covers design principles, spatial relationships, and the mindset of an architect in a way that is accessible and memorable. If you read only one book from this list, make it this one — it reinforces everything you have learned in this course.

### The Timeless Way of Building
**By Christopher Alexander**

Alexander explores architecture as a living, evolving art deeply connected to human life. He argues that the most meaningful buildings share a quality that cannot be named but can be felt — a sense of life and wholeness. This book will change how you see spaces and the patterns within them.

### Thinking Architecture
**By Peter Zumthor**

Zumthor writes about the emotional and experiential side of design — how buildings can move us, calm us, and make us feel at home. His essays explore atmosphere, memory, and the sensory qualities of materials. Reading Zumthor deepens your appreciation for why architecture matters beyond function.

### Sustainable Home
**By Christine Liu**

A practical and beautifully designed guide to incorporating sustainability into residential spaces. It covers energy efficiency, material choices, and everyday decisions that make homes healthier and more environmentally responsible. An excellent companion to the environmental design concepts from Module 5.

## Going Further

These books are starting points, not endpoints. As your interest in architecture grows, you will discover authors, movements, and ideas that resonate with your unique design voice. Visit your local library, explore architectural bookshops, and let your curiosity guide you.

Architecture is a lifelong conversation — these books are an invitation to join it.
