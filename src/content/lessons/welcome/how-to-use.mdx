# How to Use This Course

This guide will help you get the most out of your learning experience.

## Self-Paced Learning

There are no deadlines. Work through the lessons at whatever pace feels comfortable. The course follows a linear structure, so we recommend going in order, but you are free to jump around if a topic catches your eye. Some students complete a lesson per day; others binge through a module on weekends. Both approaches work great.

## Course Features

Here is what you will find inside each module:

- **Short videos** introducing key concepts and setting the stage for each section
- **Digestible written lessons** that break down architectural ideas into approachable, well-organized content
- **Design prompts and exercises** that let you apply what you learn to your own dream home or ideal space
- **Curated free resources** to explore topics further on your own
- **Project-based assignments** that build toward your final portfolio or design brief
- **Optional deeper-dive content** for students who want to explore further
- **Downloadable PDFs** including worksheets, templates, reference guides, and cheat sheets

## Track Your Progress

As you complete each lesson, click the **Mark Complete** button at the bottom. This updates your progress dashboard so you can see how far you've come.

## Path Callouts

Throughout the course, you will see exercises marked with colored path callout boxes. These indicate two different approaches to each exercise:

- **Drawer Path** (teal) — Best if you enjoy hands-on, visual work like sketching and diagramming
- **Brief Builder Path** (amber) — Best if you prefer writing, analysis, and verbal descriptions

You do not need to choose one path for the entire course. You can switch between them at any time, do one or the other, or try both on any given exercise. Most core content applies to everyone regardless of path.

## Tips for Success

- **Set up a workspace** — A dedicated spot, even a corner of a desk, helps you focus
- **Keep your notebook open** — Jot down ideas as they come to you; use the highlight feature to clip key passages as you read
- **Take breaks** — Architecture is creative work; let ideas percolate
- **Revisit lessons** — Some concepts click better the second time around
- **Develop a portfolio** — Keep your exercises, sketches, and notes together as you go through the course; by the end you will have a personal design portfolio worth holding onto
