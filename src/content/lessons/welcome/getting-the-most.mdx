# Getting the Most Out of This Course

A few recommendations from students and our team to help you get the most value from your learning experience.

## Set Up Your Environment

The course is designed to be an immersive, hands-on experience. A little setup before you dive in goes a long way.

<ProTip title="Best on Desktop">

This course works best on a **desktop or laptop computer**. The split-panel layout with lesson content on the left and tools (notebook, AI chat) on the right is designed for larger screens. That said, it also works great on the go via **tablet or mobile** for reading lessons and reviewing notes.

</ProTip>

## Pin the Course Panel

On the left side of every lesson, you will see the course navigation sidebar. We recommend **pinning it open** by clicking the pin icon at the top. This gives you a persistent view of all modules and lessons, making it easy to track where you are and jump between sections.

With the sidebar pinned, you can see your completion progress at a glance and plan which lessons to tackle next.

## Keep the Notebook Open

The tools panel on the right side of each lesson houses your notebook and AI chat. We recommend **keeping it open** while you work through lessons so that you can:

- Capture key ideas the moment they stand out to you
- Highlight important passages and add your own commentary
- Build a running collection of notes that grows with each module

<ProTip title="Why This Matters">

Your notebook is more than a scratchpad. At the end of the course, you will have the option to generate a **professional Design Brief** compiled from everything you have captured. The more notes you take along the way, the richer and more personalized your Design Brief will be.

</ProTip>

## Leverage the AI Chat

The AI study partner is not just for answering questions. Here are some ways to get real value from it:

- **After reading a lesson**, ask it to summarize the key takeaways in your own context (e.g., "How do these principles apply to a mid-century modern ranch?")
- **Before starting an exercise**, ask for guidance on how to approach it
- **When you are stuck**, describe what you are trying to achieve and ask for suggestions
- **For vocabulary**, ask it to explain any unfamiliar term with a relatable example

Think of it as a conversation with a knowledgeable friend who happens to understand architecture.

## Develop a Note-Taking Rhythm

The students who get the most from this course tend to follow a simple rhythm:

1. **Read or watch the lesson** — Focus on understanding, not memorizing
2. **Highlight key passages** — Save standout ideas to your notebook as you go
3. **Add your own thoughts** — After each lesson, jot down one or two sentences about what stood out or how it connects to your space
4. **Review after each module** — Before starting the next module, skim through your notebook entries from the one you just finished; this brief review cements the ideas and often sparks new connections

<ProTip title="Physical Notes Welcome">

If you prefer pen and paper, go for it. You can always summarize your physical notes in the course notebook later. Some students find that writing by hand during the lesson and then typing a summary afterward is the most effective approach for retention.

</ProTip>

## Have Your Materials Ready

If you are following the **Drawer Path** and plan to sketch, make sure you have your basic supplies within reach before starting a module:

- Grid paper or sketchbook
- Straightedge or ruler
- Mechanical pencil and eraser
- Colored pencils or markers (optional, helpful for exercises)

If you purchased the **Starter Kit**, keep it at your workspace. Having everything at arm's reach makes it much easier to jump into exercises without losing momentum.

## One Module at a Time

While you are free to jump around, the course is designed to build on itself. Each module introduces concepts that the next one builds upon. We recommend completing modules in order at least on your first pass. You can always revisit individual lessons later as reference material.

Most importantly, do not rush. This is not a course you need to finish in a weekend. Take your time, enjoy the process, and let the ideas develop naturally. Architecture is about thoughtful design, and that starts with how you approach learning it.
