## You Don't Need AutoCAD to Draw a Floor Plan

If you're looking for the best floor plan software for beginners, you've probably already discovered that the professional tools — AutoCAD, Revit, ArchiCAD — are wildly overkill. They take months to learn, cost hundreds per year, and are designed for licensed professionals producing construction documents.

You don't need that. You need something that lets you sketch ideas, test layouts, and communicate with your architect or builder. Something you can learn in an afternoon.

That's where [Rayon](https://rayon.design) and [Spacely AI](https://spacely.ai) come in. Both are modern, beginner-friendly floor plan tools. Both are covered in our [AI tools roundup](/blog/best-ai-tools-home-design-2026). But they take fundamentally different approaches to the same problem.

I've spent serious time with both while building the AI module for Foundations of Architecture. Here's an honest breakdown of how they compare — strengths, weaknesses, and which one you should pick.

---

## The Core Difference

Before I get into features, here's the fundamental distinction:

**Rayon** is a design tool. You draw floor plans manually using an intuitive interface. You're the designer — Rayon gives you the canvas and the tools.

**Spacely AI** is a generative tool. You tell it what you need (room count, square footage, style preferences), and it generates floor plan options using AI. You're the director — Spacely does the drafting.

This difference shapes everything. The tool that's better for *you* depends on whether you want to create from scratch or start with AI-generated suggestions and refine from there.

---

## Rayon: The Hands-On Approach

### What It Does Well

[Rayon](https://rayon.design) excels at giving beginners a professional-feeling design experience without the learning curve of professional software. You draw walls by clicking and dragging. You drop in doors, windows, and furniture from a library. The interface responds the way you'd expect — it feels like the design tools you've used in other contexts, just purpose-built for floor plans.

The standout feature is the real-time 3D preview. As you draw your floor plan in 2D, a 3D model builds itself simultaneously. You can switch to a first-person walkthrough at any point and *feel* the space you're creating. That hallway you drew? Walk through it virtually and you'll immediately know if it's too narrow.

If you understand what a [floor plan](/blog/what-is-a-floor-plan) represents — walls, doors, windows, dimensions — Rayon removes all the technical barriers to creating one yourself.

Other strengths:

- **Clean, uncluttered interface.** No feature overwhelm. You see what you need and nothing more.
- **Accurate dimensions.** It snaps to standard measurements, so your walls are realistic thicknesses and your doors are standard widths.
- **Furniture library.** Drop in sofas, tables, beds, and appliances to test whether your layout actually works with real-sized furniture — the step most beginners skip.
- **Export options.** You can export images, PDFs, and 3D files to share with architects and builders.

### Where It Falls Short

Rayon requires you to know what you want before you start drawing. There's no AI suggesting layouts or generating options. If you're staring at a blank canvas with no idea where to begin, it can feel overwhelming — not because the tool is complicated, but because design is hard when you're starting from zero.

It's also less helpful for the "what if" exploration phase. Comparing five radically different layouts means drawing five plans from scratch. That's time-consuming, even with an intuitive tool.

### Pricing

Rayon offers a free tier that covers most of what beginners need — 2D floor plans, basic furniture placement, and limited exports. Paid plans (starting around $12/month) unlock higher-resolution exports, additional 3D features, and the ability to manage multiple projects.

## Spacely AI: The AI-First Approach

### What It Does Well

[Spacely AI](https://spacely.ai) is built for the exploration phase. You describe what you need — a 1,200-square-foot, 2-bedroom apartment with an open kitchen and home office — and it generates multiple floor plan options in seconds. Each option is different, showing you layouts you might never have considered.

This is genuinely powerful. The hardest part of home design for beginners isn't the drawing — it's knowing what's possible. Spacely AI solves that by showing you a range of solutions before you've committed to anything.

The AI suggestions are also educational. You'll see layouts where the kitchen connects to the dining area in unexpected ways, or where the bedroom placement creates privacy you wouldn't have thought to design. It's like brainstorming with a fast, tireless design assistant.

Other strengths:

- **Speed.** Going from requirements to multiple floor plan options takes seconds, not hours.
- **Prompt-based interface.** You type what you need in natural language. No learning curve for the input side.
- **Style-aware suggestions.** You can specify design preferences (modern, traditional, open concept) and the AI adjusts its output accordingly.
- **Interior design visualization.** Beyond floor plans, Spacely can generate interior design concepts — helping you visualize how a room might look furnished and styled.

### Where It Falls Short

AI-generated floor plans are starting points, not final designs. The layouts Spacely produces are often conceptually interesting but need significant refinement. Dimensions might be slightly off. Room proportions can feel awkward. The AI doesn't always understand practical constraints like structural walls, plumbing locations, or building code requirements.

The editing tools for refining AI-generated plans are less mature than Rayon's purpose-built drawing tools. Once you have an AI suggestion you like, tweaking it precisely can feel clunky compared to working in a dedicated drafting environment.

There's also a creative limitation: AI generates based on patterns in its training data. It's great at producing conventional layouts but less likely to suggest the truly creative, site-specific solution that a thoughtful human designer might develop.

### Pricing

Spacely AI offers free trial generations so you can test it out. Full access requires a subscription (starting around $15/month), which includes unlimited generations, higher-resolution outputs, and access to their full feature set including interior visualization.

## Which One Should You Use?

Here's my honest recommendation based on where you are in the process:

**Use Spacely AI if:**

- You're in the early exploration phase and don't have a clear layout in mind
- You want to see a range of options quickly to understand what's possible
- You're comparing different room configurations (2-bed vs. 3-bed, open plan vs. traditional)
- You want quick visual concepts to share in early conversations with family or designers

**Use Rayon if:**

- You have a general layout concept and want to draw it precisely
- You need accurate dimensions and realistic room sizes
- You want to test furniture placement to confirm a layout actually works
- You need clean exports to share with an architect or builder
- You want to iterate on a specific design, making incremental improvements

**Use both if:**

- You start with Spacely to generate and explore layout ideas
- Then move to Rayon to redraw your favorite concept with precision, test it with furniture, and produce something worth sharing with a professional

This two-tool workflow is actually what I teach in the FOA Course AI module. They complement each other — Spacely for divergent thinking, Rayon for convergent refinement.

## What About Rendering?

Neither Rayon nor Spacely AI is a rendering tool. If you want photorealistic images of your design, you'll need a separate tool like [D5 Render](https://www.d5render.com). D5 takes 3D models and produces magazine-quality visualizations in real time — but it requires a 3D model to start with, which means it sits later in the workflow.

For most beginners, floor plan tools are the priority. Rendering comes after you've nailed the layout. Don't get distracted by pretty pictures before you've solved the functional problems.

## Neither Tool Replaces Understanding

Here's the thing I always come back to: tools are only as good as the knowledge behind them. Spacely AI can generate a dozen floor plans, but if you don't understand traffic flow, zone relationships, or spatial proportions, you can't tell which one actually works for your life. Rayon can draw anything you imagine, but if you don't understand what makes a good layout, you'll draw beautiful plans that function poorly.
